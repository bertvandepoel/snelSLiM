About snelSLiM

The first rough version of SnelSLiM was developed as part of the Bachelor Paper of Bert Van de Poel under supervision of Prof. dr. Dirk Speelman at the University of Leuven, Faculty of Arts, Department of Linguistcs. A statistically sound and feature rich version with a focus on user experience and research requirements was developed as part of the Master Thesis of Bert Van de Poel, again under the supervision of Prof. dr. Dirk Speelman. Development currently continues as part of an Advanced Master Thesis by Bert Van de Poel. This software aims to make Stable Lexical Marker Analysis quick and painless for researchers who might be less skilled with computers and statistics, while also giving those with more experience a faster and more convenient way of quickly getting to the required information.

Most of the heavy lifting is done by a set of go applications that depending on the format may use FOSS tools such as xmllint and foliafolie.
